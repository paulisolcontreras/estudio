📏 Measures: Length 📏
Leonor's Study Guide – Unit 3

⭐ Key Words to Know

centimetre (cm)
A small unit of length. About the width of your finger! 100 cm = 1 metre.
metre (m)
A big unit of length. As long as a guitar or a door is tall!
ruler
A tool we use to measure and draw lines in centimetres.
length
How long something is from one end to the other (left → right).
height
How tall something is (bottom → top). ↕
width
How wide something is from side to side. ↔
distance
How far apart two places or things are.
estimate
Your best guess before measuring. Don't use a ruler!
just over / just under
"Just over 5 cm" = a tiny bit more than 5. "Just under" = a tiny bit less.

📐 How to Use a Ruler

A ruler in centimetres looks like this:

Each big gap = 1 centimetre (cm)

1️⃣
Always put the 0 (or the end of the ruler) at the beginning of the line or object. Not the 1!
👀
Look at where the line or bar ends. That number is the measurement.
✏️
To draw a line of 5 cm: put the ruler down, make a dot at 0 and a dot at 5, then connect them!
🤔
To estimate, look and think — do NOT use your ruler. Then measure to check!

📏 When do we use cm? When do we use m?

centimetres (cm)
Use for small things you can hold!
✏️ a pencil
📏 a ruler
🍫 a bar of chocolate
👟 your shoe
🐛 a worm
📎 a paper clip
🍎 an apple
🍓 a strawberry
metres (m)
Use for big things — too big for a ruler!
🏊 a swimming pool
🚌 a bus
🧱 a wall
🚗 a car
🌳 a tall tree
🚪 a door
🛏️ your bedroom
🏫 your classroom
🌟 Quick trick: If it fits on your desk and you can measure it with a ruler → centimetres.
If it's bigger than you → metres!

📚 Important Rules to Remember

📏
When we measure → use your ruler. When we estimate → do NOT use your ruler. Guess first, then check!
0️⃣
Always start measuring from 0. Look carefully — where does the bar or line begin?
↕️
Length = how long (left to right). Height = how tall (up and down). Width = how wide (side to side).
🔢
To order lengths from shortest to longest: find the smallest number first!
➕
To find how much longer one line is: subtract! (longer – shorter = difference)
📐
The length of an object is usually longer than the width.

🏆 Quick Summary – What I Need to Know

✅ Measure objects using a ruler in centimetres — start from 0!

✅ Draw lines by putting the ruler at 0 and marking the right number.

✅ Use centimetres for small things (pencil, shoe, straw).

✅ Use metres for big things (pool, bus, room, tree).

✅ Estimate first (no ruler!), then measure to check.
